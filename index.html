An Introduction to Cricket

Cricket is a bat and ball sport played between two teams, with 11 players in each team. It originated in England and is now one of the most popular sports globally, especially in countries like India, Australia, and England. It is also the 2nd most popular sport in the World.

Comparing Baseball and Cricket

  • In Cricket, players run back and forth on a rectangular pitch to count runs. However, in Baseball, players run through bases arranged at the corners in a rhombus shape.
  • Some ways to get out in Baseball
    1. Strikeout: batter accumulates 3 strikes (misses the ball)
    2. Flyout: ball gets hit by batter and gets caught by a fielder
    3. Groundout: ball gets fielded by fielder and is thrown to first base before batter can reach
    4. Force out: Runner at first, but batter doesn't get a good hit. So, runner at first is forced to run, but batter gets out
    5. Double Play: two runners are out when the ielder throws the ball to both bases before the runners can reach
  • Some ways to get out in Cricket
    1. Catch out: fielder catches ball hit by batsman
    2. Bowl out: the ball hits the stumps (3 sticks behind the batsman)
    3. Run out: ball is thrown and hits stumps before batter can reach
    4. LBW: Ball hits batsman's legs when they are covering the stumps
    5. Stumpout: If batsman steps out, wicketkeeper can grab the ball and hit the stumps to get the batsman out
  • The balls are very similar in Cricket and Baseball, but players in Cricket do not use gloves to catch the ball
  • Cricket games are much longer, with some lasting days
